Abstract

In this paper, we study the possibility of imitating the functionality of image-to-image translation models. We focus on cases where re-training the model is impossible. For example, commercial models for biological usages which are usually invested by significant resources and therefore are considered very confidential. We show that imitation of such a model's functionality is possible using a simple known distillation approach and a single input and output image from the model. Such images are usually shared in the company's websites (demo) or in a related published paper and can be easily extracted. T through an extensive ablation study on a wide variety of model architectures, datasets and tasks, we find that many image-to-image translation models can be imitated with as little as a single, small-sized, input-output pair.
